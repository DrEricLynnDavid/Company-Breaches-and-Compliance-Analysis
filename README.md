# **The Engine: Data Breach Analysis Workbook**  
*A Supplementary Dataset for “Examining Differences between Companies that are Compliant and Have Experienced a Data Breach”*

## **Overview**
**The Engine** is the full analysis workbook developed to support the research study:

**Examining Differences between Companies that are Compliant and Have Experienced a Data Breach**  
Eric L. David, PhD (2026)

This workbook contains the complete dataset, derived metrics, timing calculations, and statistical outputs used in the published manuscript. It is provided as an open, transparent resource for researchers, practitioners, and cybersecurity professionals interested in compliance behavior, breach timing, and organizational risk patterns.

The workbook is fully anonymized and constructed from publicly available breach records, regulatory filings, and open‑source cybersecurity databases.

---

## **Contents of the Workbook**
The Engine includes:

### **1. Core Dataset**
- 204 unique breach entries  
- Industry classification (Technology, Healthcare, Finance)  
- Company metadata (public status, acquisition status, net worth, etc.)  
- Compliance indicators (GDPR, ISO, PCI, HIPAA, SOX, etc.)

### **2. Derived Timing Metrics**
These metrics were introduced in the study and are fully calculated in the workbook:

- **B2A** – Breach to Acquisition  
- **B2P** – Breach to Public (IPO)  
- **B2GDPR** – Breach to GDPR Certification  
- **B2D** – Breach to Defunct  
- **RCA** – Root Cause Analysis  
- **RCA2GDPR** – Root Cause to GDPR Certification  

These metrics enable longitudinal analysis of breach timing relative to major organizational events.

### **3. Root Cause & Data Classification Flags**
- PII, PCI, PHI exposure  
- Technical data exposure  
- Company data exposure  
- Undisclosed breach indicators  
- System vulnerability flags

### **4. Statistical Outputs**
- T‑tests  
- Mean interval calculations  
- Certification density comparisons  
- Reporting frequency analysis  
- Sector‑specific exposure patterns  

These outputs directly support the findings reported in the manuscript.

- Promote **research transparency**

- Support **future research** on compliance, breach timing, and governance costs  
- Provide a structured dataset for **machine learning**, **risk modeling**, or **comparative studies**  
- Offer practitioners a practical tool for **M&A due diligence**, **audit planning**, and **technical debt assessment**

## **How to Use The Engine**
You may:

- Import the workbook into Excel, Python, R, or Power BI  
- Reproduce the statistical tests from the paper  
- Extend the dataset with additional breach records  
- Build predictive models using the timing metrics  
- Conduct cross‑industry or cross‑framework comparisons  

The workbook is intentionally structured to be modular and extensible.
